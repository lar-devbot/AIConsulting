## Cash Flow Considerations

### Important: Timing of Cash vs. Profit

**Tier 2 Typical Flow**

```
Week 1-2: Kickoff
  - Invoice client $15k deposit (50%)
  - Cash in: $7,500

Week 3-4: Design phase
  - Invoice remaining $7,500 on delivery
  - No cash yet (awaiting invoice payment)

Week 5-6: Build phase
  - Months 1-3 retainer invoiced upfront: $15k
  - Cash in: $7,500 (remaining project) + $15k (retainer) = $22,500

Week 7-8: Launch phase
  - Months 4-6 retainer invoiced: $15k
  - Cash in: $15k

Typical payment terms: Net 30 (payment arrives 30 days after invoice)
```

**Recommendation**: 
- Invoice 50% upfront (project start)
- Invoice 50% at delivery (reduces your cash float)
- Monthly retainers invoiced upfront (30-day net)

This keeps you cash-positive even as you scale.

## Pricing Strategy Justification

### Why $15k Project + $5k/Month?

**Compared to hourly consulting:**
- Hourly rate for similar work: $150-200/hr
- This project at $150/hr = $24,000+ (not $15k)
- **You're offering 40% discount** vs. hourly

**Compared to competitors:**
- Tier 3 AI agencies: $3-8k/month (no upfront cost)
- Enterprise AI consulting: $100k+ minimum
- **You're in the sweet spot:** More professional than agencies, more affordable than enterprise

**Compared to client ROI:**
- Typical client saves $30-50k/year in labor
- Investment: $15k + $60k/year retainer = $75k total
- ROI: 40-66% in Year 1 (breaks even in 7-8 months)
- **Extremely attractive client ROI**

### Why Outcome-Based Isn't the Right Model (Yet)

**Why not "pay 20% of savings"?**
- Harder to predict savings (what if client doesn't use the system?)
- Client has incentive to underreport savings (to pay you less)
- Slower to scale (you get paid over time, not upfront)
- Better once you have track record + case studies

**Better approach for now:**
- Fixed pricing (predictability for both sides)
- Performance monitoring (prove value, build trust)
- Upgrade to outcome-based in Year 2 (once you have data)

## Financial Assumptions & Sensitivity

### Key Assumptions

1. **Sales conversion**: 30-40% of qualified prospects convert to Tier 2
2. **Sales cycle**: 4-6 weeks from first call to signature
3. **Average deal value**: $5k/month retainer
4. **Concurrent clients**: 5-6 (realistic max for one person)
5. **Churn**: 10% annually (clients leave or scale down)
6. **Gross margin**: 55-70% (improves with scale)

### Sensitivity Analysis

**If conversion rate drops to 20%:**
- Slower growth (Month 9 = $15k instead of $28k)
- Still profitable by Month 6

**If average deal value is $3k/month (not $5k):**
- Need 8-10 concurrent clients to hit $30k
- Harder to manage; quality suffers
- Recommend: Focus on higher-value deals vs. more deals

**If you can only handle 3-4 clients (part-time):**
- Target: $15-20k/month (still excellent part-time income)
- Path: $0 → $15k in 4-5 months

## Long-Term Revenue Scenarios (Year 2+)

### Scenario A: Stable (Repeat Revenue Focus)

```
Year 2:
- Retain 80% of Year 1 clients (4-5 clients, ~$20k/mo base)
- Add 2-3 new clients for growth
```

### Scenario B: Growth (Upsells + Tier 3 Focus)

```
Year 2:
- Retain 80% of Year 1 clients (4-5 clients, ~$20k/mo base)
- Convert 50% to Tier 3 upgrades (2-3 clients, add $3k/mo each = $9k/mo)

Year 3:
- Repeat with higher base
- Total: $60-80k/month

Advantage: Exponential growth, higher margins, fewer clients
Disadvantage: Requires strong positioning + sales execution
```

```
Year 2:
- You handle 5-6 top clients ($30k/mo)
- Hire junior consultant (handles 3-4 simpler clients, $15k/mo)
- You take 30% of junior's revenue = $4.5k/mo
- Total: $34.5k/mo (team revenue), $25k/mo (your margin)

Year 3:
- 2 junior consultants
- Total: $60k+/mo (team), $40k+/mo (your margin)

Advantage: Unlimited scale, build an agency
Disadvantage: Management overhead, quality control, hiring
```
